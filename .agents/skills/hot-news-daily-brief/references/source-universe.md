# Source Universe Checklist

Use this file as a coverage checklist, not a strict whitelist.
Prefer primary and authoritative sources whenever possible.

## Mainstream Global News

Prioritize several from this group every run:
- Reuters
- Associated Press (AP)
- BBC
- The New York Times
- The Wall Street Journal
- Financial Times
- Bloomberg
- Al Jazeera

## Finance and Markets

Prioritize several from this group every run:
- Bloomberg Markets
- Reuters Business/Markets
- Financial Times Markets
- Wall Street Journal Markets
- CNBC
- MarketWatch
- Nikkei Asia

## Technology and Product News

Prioritize several from this group every run:
- TechCrunch
- The Verge
- Wired
- Ars Technica
- MIT Technology Review
- Hacker News (community signal, not sole authority)

## Social and Community Signals

Use as trend detectors, then verify with credible sources:
- Reddit:
  - r/news
  - r/worldnews
  - r/technology
  - r/artificial
- X / Twitter:
  - Official organization accounts
  - Recognized journalists and analysts
  - Industry leaders (for original announcements)

## Chinese Content and Community Signals

Use for China-centric trend detection and sentiment:
- Xiaohongshu (小红书)
- Weibo Hot Search (fallback if Xiaohongshu data is limited)
- Zhihu hot topics (supplement)

## Evidence to Capture for "Hotness"

Collect at least one of these signals when available:
- Headline placement (homepage, top story, breaking).
- Engagement metrics (upvotes, comments, reposts, likes, discussion velocity).
- Cross-source replication speed (how fast other credible outlets picked it up).
- Opinion leader amplification (well-known journalists, analysts, or public figures).

## Reliability Rules

- Never treat social virality as sufficient evidence by itself.
- Confirm high-impact claims with at least one authoritative outlet.
- Label unconfirmed details explicitly or exclude them.
